# On Becoming Nabalus

A birthday card for a twenty-first, told through one woodland plant — **Nabalus**, the species once classified as *Prenanthes*.

The card uses two true things about the plant as a coming-of-age metaphor:

1. **Its leaves change shape as it matures** (a trait called *heteroblasty*). On a single plant, the lowest leaves are broad and undivided while the upper leaves are deeply palmate — the same self, almost unrecognizable from one stage to the next. The four leaf photos here are arranged as that progression: undivided → first lobes → three-lobed → deeply palmate.
2. **It outgrew its name.** Long placed in the genus *Prenanthes*, the North American species were segregated into *Nabalus* by the botanist Henri Cassini in 1825; later molecular work confirmed the split.

A plant, in other words, that changed both its leaves and its name on the way to becoming itself.

## Viewing it

Open `index.html` in any browser. It's self-contained (the only external dependency is Google Fonts, which degrades gracefully to a system serif offline). It reveals gently as you scroll, and prints cleanly to PDF (File → Print → Save as PDF).

If GitHub Pages is enabled for this repo, it's also live at:
**https://chrysogonum.github.io/nabalus-card/**

## Contents

- `index.html` — the card (design, poem, message, botanical note)
- `images/` — web-optimized leaf photographs and the social-preview image

The full-resolution original photographs are kept locally but excluded from the repository (see `.gitignore`) to keep it lightweight.

## The poem

> **On Becoming Nabalus**
>
> You began as one whole leaf, / round and unwritten, / a single soft intention of green —
> the self before the self has edges, / the face the years have not yet cut.
>
> *(continues in the card)*
