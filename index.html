  • I

    Cantharophily

    Magnolia grandiflora

    An epic in deep time, for a magnolia in a jar of tap water — the flower as the first engagement engine, the beetle it had to flatter, and the long line that runs from a Cretaceous bowl to the feed in your hand.

  • II

    Snakeroot

    Nabalus — once Prenanthes

    A charm for becoming, after Appalachian folklore — a woodland plant that changes the shape of its leaves as it grows up, and once shed its own name. A coming-of-age, told in rattlesnake-root and gall-of-the-earth.

Colophon
